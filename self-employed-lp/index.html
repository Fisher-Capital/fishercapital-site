What I Can Look At With You

Four paths that come up most often for self-employed borrowers

Bank Statement Qualifying
Some B-lenders qualify income on 12 months of business account deposits instead of your T1. If cash flow is consistent and documented, this reflects what you actually earn — not what the CRA sees after write-offs.
2-Year Income Averaging
Many alternative lenders allow income to be averaged over two years. A single low year doesn't kill the application if the overall picture is stable. Useful when income fluctuates year-to-year.
Stated Income with Support
Some lenders accept stated income backed by contracts, invoices, or business financials — without depending exclusively on CRA-reported net income. Documentation requirements vary by lender and loan amount.
B-Lender Access
Regulated alternative lenders exist specifically for complex income profiles. They're not payday lenders — they're FSRA-regulated institutions with real mortgage products. Rates are higher than a chartered bank. It's a different lane, not a last resort.
