The Problem

Where self-employed files break down

The bank used the wrong number

They qualified you on net income after deductions — line 15000 on your T1 General. The more business expenses you've written off, the smaller that number looks. Your actual cash flow could be three times higher and it doesn't matter to them.

You haven't been self-employed long enough

Major banks typically want two full years of self-employment history. If you're at 14 months, or if year two shows a dip, the application often doesn't clear their system. Not every lender draws the line in the same place.

Corporation owners run into a wall

If you pay yourself via dividends or leave income in the corporation, your personal T4 can look minimal. Banks see minimal personal income and stop there. Some lenders look at the full picture — retained earnings, distributions, business revenue — rather than just what flowed to you personally.

What Tends to Exist

Approaches for self-employed files

Every file is different. These are the paths that commonly come up — whether any apply to you depends on your specific situation.

Bank Statement Qualifying
Some B-lenders qualify income based on 12 months of deposits into your business account rather than relying on your T1 General. If your cash flow is consistent and documented, this can reflect your actual income far more accurately than a tax return shaped by write-offs.
2-Year Income Averaging
Many alternative lenders allow self-employment income to be averaged over two years. A single low year doesn't disqualify you if the trend is stable. This is also how some deal with income that varies significantly between years.
Stated Income with Support
Some lenders allow income to be stated with supporting documentation — contracts, invoices, business financial statements, or NOAs — without relying exclusively on CRA-reported net income. The documentation requirements vary by lender and loan amount.
B-Lender Access
If A-lender criteria don't fit, regulated alternative lenders exist specifically for complex income profiles. Their rates are higher than a chartered bank, but they're real, regulated lenders with real mortgage products. This isn't a last resort — it's a different lane.
